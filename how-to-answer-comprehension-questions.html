How To Answer Comprehension Questions

May 2024 · 3 minute read

Comprehension questions are an essential part of reading and testing your understanding of a text. Whether you’re studying for an exam, preparing for an interview, or simply trying to improve your reading comprehension skills, knowing how to answer comprehension questions effectively is key. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of how to answer comprehension questions with confidence and precision.

Before attempting to answer any comprehension questions, take the time to read the passage carefully. Pay close attention to the main ideas, key details, and any supporting evidence presented in the text. Try to grasp the overall meaning and purpose of the passage.

  • Once you’ve read the passage, identify the main idea or central theme. Ask yourself what the passage is primarily about and what message or argument the author is conveying. Look for recurring themes, concepts, or keywords that can help you pinpoint the main idea.
  • Scan the passage again to locate specific details, examples, or evidence that support the main idea. Pay attention to any dates, names, statistics, or quotations mentioned in the text. Highlight or underline relevant information that you may need to reference when answering the comprehension questions.
  • Before answering each comprehension question, carefully analyze the question stem to understand what is being asked. Identify keywords or phrases that provide clues about the type of information you need to look for in the passage. Pay attention to question words like “who,” “what,” “where,” “when,” “why,” and “how.”
  • Refer back to the passage to find the information needed to answer the question. Use the details and evidence you identified earlier to support your response. Take your time to locate the relevant section of the passage and read it thoroughly to ensure accuracy.
  • When choosing your answer, carefully evaluate each option and eliminate any choices that are clearly incorrect or irrelevant. Cross out these options to narrow down your selection and increase your chances of choosing the correct answer. Use the process of elimination to make educated guesses if you’re unsure.
  • Pay attention to contextual clues within the passage that can help you infer the meaning of unfamiliar words or phrases. Look for surrounding sentences or paragraphs that provide context or clarification. Use your critical thinking skills to make logical deductions based on the information presented.
  • When answering comprehension questions, strive to be concise and clear in your responses. Avoid providing unnecessary details or overcomplicating your answers. Focus on addressing the specific requirements of the question and providing a well-supported response based on evidence from the passage.
  • Before submitting your answers, take the time to review and revise your responses. Double-check for any errors, inconsistencies, or omissions that may need correction. Ensure that your answers are accurate, coherent, and aligned with the requirements of each question.
  • Improving your ability to answer comprehension questions takes practice and persistence. Dedicate time to regularly practice reading passages and answering related questions. Challenge yourself with a variety of passages from different genres and topics to strengthen your skills over time.
