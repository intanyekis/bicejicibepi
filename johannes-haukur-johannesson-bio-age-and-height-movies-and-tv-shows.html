BIOGRAPHY OF JOHANNES HAUKUR JOHANNESSON

Jóhannes Haukur Jóhannesson was born in 1980. He is an Icelandic actor known for his roles in the series Game of Thrones and The Innocents and in the films Atomic Blonde and Alpha. Jóhannes was born and raised in Iceland but spent some of his childhood years in the Faroe Islands with his mother.

He is fluent in Icelandic, Faroese, Danish and English. He received a BA in Theater from the Iceland Academy of Arts in 2005 and has since worked in theatre, television and film. He has extensive theater experience in Iceland and was artist-in-residence at the National Theater until he started working in international television and film projects.

His first international film role was in Darren Aronofsky’s ‘Noah’ as ​​Cain (the world’s first murderer). His first international television role was on NBC’s ‘AD: Kingdom And Empire’ as Apostle Thomas. His TV credits also include BBC’s Game of Thrones and The Last Kingdom.
