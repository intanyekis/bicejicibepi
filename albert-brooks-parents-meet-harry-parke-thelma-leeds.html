Albert Brooks’ parents, Thelma Leeds (née Goodman) and Harry Einstein, were integral threads in the showbiz tapestry of their time. Thelma Leeds, a gifted actress, contributed to the vibrancy of the entertainment industry. Meanwhile, Harry Einstein, known by his stage name “Parkyakarkus,” was a radio comedian who left an indelible mark with his performances on Eddie Cantor’s radio program.

Harry Einstein: The Radio Luminary:

Albert’s father, Harry Einstein, played a pivotal role in the golden age of radio. His comedic prowess shone through on Eddie Cantor’s radio program, where he delighted audiences with his distinctive humor. Adopting the stage name “Parkyakarkus,” Harry Einstein became a household name, contributing to the laughter that echoed through the airwaves.

Thelma Leeds: The Actress:

Albert Brooks’ mother, Thelma Leeds, added her layer of talent to the family’s show business legacy. As an actress, she graced the stage and screen, showcasing her skills in the performing arts. Thelma Leeds illuminated the world of entertainment with her performances and played a crucial role in nurturing Albert’s passion for the craft.
